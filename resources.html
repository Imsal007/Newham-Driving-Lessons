"Show Me, Tell Me" Questions

Vehicle safety check questions with step-by-step answers.

Pro Tip

"Show me" questions require physical demonstration. "Tell me" questions are verbal only. Practice both until they are second nature!

"Tell Me" Questions (Before Driving)

  • How do you check the brakes are working properly?
  • How do you ensure the head restraint is correctly adjusted?
  • How would you check the tyres have sufficient tread depth?
  • How do you check the lights are working?
  • How would you check the windscreen wipers and washers?

"Show Me" Questions (While Driving)

  • Show me how you'd switch on dipped headlights
  • Show me how you'd set the rear fog lights
  • Show me how you'd demist the front windscreen
  • Show me how you'd use the horn
  • Show me how you'd clean the front windscreen

Manoeuvres Mastery Guide

Step-by-step instructions for every test manoeuvre.

Parallel Park (Right Side)

  1. Check mirrors and signal left to pull up alongside the vehicle (2 car lengths ahead).
  2. Position parallel, about 1 metre away.
  3. Select reverse, check all around including blind spots.
  4. Steer fully right as you reverse until you see the number plate in your left door mirror.
  5. Straighten steering and reverse until your front door aligns with their rear bumper.
  6. Steer fully left and continue reversing into the space.
  7. Straighten wheels when parallel to the curb, leaving ~30cm gap.

Bay Park (Reverse In)

  1. Approach slowly, positioning 2 car lengths from the bay lines.
  2. Align your shoulder with the second line of the bay.
  3. Check mirrors, steer fully towards the bay as you move forward past it.
  4. Stop when shoulders align with the far end of the bay.
  5. Select reverse, check all around.
  6. Steer fully into the bay and reverse slowly, adjusting as needed.
  7. Use mirrors to check you're within the lines, then straighten wheels.

Hazard Perception Mastery

Learn what counts as a developing hazard and perfect your timing.

How It Works

  • 14 video clips showing real road scenarios
  • 15 developing hazards total (one clip has 2)
  • Maximum 5 points per hazard
  • Pass mark: 44 out of 75

What Is a Developing Hazard?

Something that would cause you to take action – slowing down, stopping, or changing direction. It's actively developing and requires your response.

Perfect Your Timing

Click too early = zero points. Click too late = fewer points. The ideal window is when the hazard starts to develop but before it becomes critical.

Test Day Preparation

Reduce anxiety and ensure success. Know exactly what to expect.

Pre-Test Checklist

  • Provisional driving licence (photocard)
  • Theory test pass certificate
  • Glasses/contacts if required
  • Arrive 10 minutes early

Top 10 Common Failures

  • Inadequate mirror checks
  • Poor observation at junctions
  • Incorrect positioning at roundabouts
  • Steering control issues
  • Incorrect speed for conditions
